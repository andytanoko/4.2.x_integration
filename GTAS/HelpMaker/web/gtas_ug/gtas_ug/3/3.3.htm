3.3 Navigation Panel

The navigation panel presents a hierarchical list of links to various functions of the application. An example of a function can be a certain list view page or other types of screens like Company Profile page, etc. Clicking (single clicking) the underlined text causes the associated screen to be displayed in the panel on the right. The links that are displayed in the navigation panel depend on which of the available tabs at the top of the screen is selected. The tabs are selected by clicking them (a single click), and it can take a second or two for the new screen to be displayed.

Some links have a small plus (+) sign in a little box next to them. You can click on this plus sign to reveal hidden links. You will notice that when you move the mouse pointer arrow over the symbol, it will itself change to indicate that this is clickable. Unlike the hand symbol used for links, a plus symbol will be shown. Like links, these objects require only a single click to activate. Once expanded, the plus symbol will change to a minus symbol (-) to indicate that another click will hide the revealed links again.
