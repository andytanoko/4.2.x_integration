3.6.3 Checkboxes and Radio Buttons

A checkbox is a small box that may contain a checkmark. Checkboxes may be in one of two states: checked or unchecked. Usually a checked value will correspond with a “true” or “yes” meaning. To change the value of a checkbox, simply click the box. If it is checked, the check will be removed. If it is not checked, it will become checked.

Radio buttons are similar to checkboxes, but they work as a group. They are usually rendered with a round shape by most browsers. Among a group of radio buttons, only a single radio button may be checked at any one time. When you click the radio button corresponding to the option you want, the previously selected radio button will be unchecked.
