6.3.1 To view the outbound folder

Click “Outbound” in the navigation tree (accessed by clicking the “Documents” tab). This will bring you to Outbound Documents folder where you can delete business documents in the table. The columns in the table represent the following information:

GridDoc ID:

It is the unique ID number in the Outbound Folder used for tracking purposes.

Has Attachments:

Indicates whether the business document has any files attached. If Yes, it means the business document has attached files. If No, the business document has no attached files.

Doc. Type:

Type of the business document. For example, 34A Purchase Order Request, Unclassified, etc.

Doc. No:

Document number (if applicable) of the business document.

Recipient Partner ID:

Recipient’s Partner ID as defined by the sender.

Doc. Filename:

Filename of the business document. For example, PO.xml

Audit File:

Filename of the audit document. For example, 3C3.xml

Transaction Status:

The transaction status of the business document. For example, “processed”

Channel Name:

Name of the channel the document is sent via as defined by the sender.

Route via:

This indicates whether the document is directly sent to partner or routed via some other means to partner.

Date/Time Sent:

Time/date when sending of the document began.

Date/Time Transacted:

Time/date when document is received at the recipient partner and processed at the Inbound Folder. For RosettaNet documents, this means an RosettaNet acknowledgment receipt has been returned from the recipient partner.

User Tracking ID:

User tracking identifier extracted from the business document, if any.

Process Instance UID:

Process Instance ID of the process the business document participates in. This is unique with respect to the initiator of the business process.
