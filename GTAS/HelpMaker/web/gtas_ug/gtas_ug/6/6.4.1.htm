6.4.1 To view the inbound folder

Click “Inbound” in the Document navigation tree. This will bring you to the Inbound Documents folder where you can delete business documents in the table. The columns in the table represent the following information:

GridDoc ID:

It is the unique ID number in the Inbound Folder used for tracking purposes.

Has Attachments:

Indicates whether the business document has any files attached. If Yes, it means the business document has attached files. If No, the business document has no attached files.

Doc. Type:

Document type (as defined by user) of the business document.

Doc. No:

Document number (if any) of the business document.

Sender Partner ID:

Sender’s partner ID, as defined by receiver.

Doc. Filename:

Filename of the business document.

Audit File:

Filename of the audit document.

Transaction Status:

The transaction status of the business document.

Route via:

This indicates whether the document is directly received from partner or routed via GridMaster from partner.

Date/Time Received:

Time/date when document is fully received in the folder.

User Tracking ID:

User tracking identifier extracted from the business document, if any.

Process Instance UID:

Process Instance ID of the process the business document participates in. This is unique with respect to the initiator of the business process.
